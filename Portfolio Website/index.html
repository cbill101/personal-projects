About Me

Thanks for dropping by... My name is Chris Billingsley. I'm a Sophomore at the University of Utah. I'm currently a CS major. This is surprising to me, as I have considered a completely different path several years ago. My desire to go into CS emerged very recently, when I was a middle school student. That was the time when I took my very first programming class. After that, despite my frustrations, I knew the CS field was where I wanted to be.

Before high school, I wanted to go into the medical field. I was one of the cookie cutter kids who wanted to be a doctor. I also thought about being a pharmacist. At some point, I even thought about becoming a police officer!

That all changed.

It all refers back to my love for gaming. I played on several consoles in the past, such as the PS1, Nintendo 64, Xbox 360, and PS4. However, I started to discover PC gaming. In the process, I did a LOT of research. This research consisted of learning new hardware knowledge, figuring out compatibilities, and other things. I eventually became obsessed with researching this to the point where I learned about why all of these components exist: their purposes, fucntions, roles in the team of hardware working to make today's modern marvel: the computer.

After all of this research, I naturally thought about how games were made. I wanted to know how they were created. I wanted to know how it all came together. Thus, my programming journey began. In 9th grade, I decided to learn a programming language: Python. It was a very simple language to learn, and I enjoyed it. I have not coded in Python for a long time, but it should be easy to pick up again. In high school, I learned bits and pieces of C# and Java, in formal programming courses. In contrast to Python, I did not like how I learned those languages initially. I felt like I was held back and not properly taught. As a result, I learned the majority of programming concepts on my own, and learned what and what not to do along the way. My projects will demonstrate my frustration from past courses. I made many mistakes that I didn't know WERE mistakes. However, that is all a part of learning.
